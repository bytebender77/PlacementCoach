PlacementCoach

AI-Powered Career Intelligence for Indian Students

Seed Stage EdTech · CareerTech India · B2C → B2B2C MVP: 7–10 Days
01 / Problem Statement

⚠ The Problem

Every year, 5+ million Indian college students enter placement season with almost zero reliable guidance. They rely on seniors, YouTube, and WhatsApp forwards — fragmented, outdated, and not personalized.

🧠 Emotional Pain
  • "Mera CGPA 7.2 hai — kya milega mujhe?"
  • Constant comparison with peers
  • Fear of being left behind
  • No one to give honest feedback
📋 Practical Pain
  • Resume gets rejected — no idea why
  • No structured upskilling path
  • Students apply blindly to 50+ companies
  • No feedback loop after rejection

Why Existing Solutions Fail

  • Resume builders (Novoresume, Zety) — generic, not India-aware, no ATS context for Indian JDs
  • Career counsellors — expensive (₹5,000–₹20,000), opinionated, not scalable
  • College TPOs — under-resourced, outdated industry connections, no personalization
  • LinkedIn/YouTube — content overload, no personalized direction

Core Insight: Students don't lack information — they lack personalized, actionable direction. That gap is the product.

02 / Ideal Customer Profile

👤 Target Users

Primary User

2nd–4th year B.Tech / BCA / MBA students in India. Tier 1, 2 & 3 colleges. Age 19–24. Actively preparing for placements in next 3–12 months.

Secondary User

Fresh graduates (0–1 yr). Lateral switchers (1–3 yr exp). Students seeking internships. Bootcamp grads with no college network.

Mindset

Aspirational. Anxious. Mobile-first. Influenced by peers. Price-sensitive but pays for outcomes. Active on WhatsApp, Instagram, LinkedIn.

The Behavioral Trigger

The product must catch users at peak anxiety: after a rejection, before placement season starts, or when a friend lands an offer. These are the moments they will sign up, share, and pay.

Key insight: Tier 2 and Tier 3 college students are chronically underserved. They have high placement anxiety but near-zero access to premium guidance. This is the core market — not IITians who already have a network.

03 / Solution

💡 What We're Building

"Don't just predict your chances — improve them."
PlacementCoach analyzes your profile, identifies the gap, and builds you a personalized roadmap to get placed.

PlacementCoach is an AI-driven career improvement platform. A student uploads their resume, enters their CGPA and skills, and receives: a realistic placement probability, an ATS resume score, and a week-by-week action plan to close the gap.

The transformation: A confused 3rd-year student with a 7.0 CGPA walks in. They leave with a clear 6-week roadmap — which projects to build, which topics to study, which companies to target. They come back weekly to track progress.

04 / Core Features (MVP)

🧩 What We Ship First

📊 Placement Reality Check MUST HAVE

User enters CGPA, college tier, skills. Gets a probabilistic score (not a hard number — shown as a range like 40–60%) broken down by role type: SDE, Data Analyst, Product, Core. Also shows: "Students with similar profiles got X, Y, Z."

Why it works: hooks the user emotionally. Creates the "oh no, I need to fix this" moment.

🧾 AI Resume Analyzer MUST HAVE

Upload resume (PDF). Get: ATS compatibility score, missing keywords per job role, section-by-section feedback (weak summary, missing metrics, formatting issues), and rewrite suggestions for bullet points.

Why it works: first tool that gives users instant, honest feedback. Builds trust fast.

🎯 Personalized Action Plan MUST HAVE

Based on gap analysis: generates a 4–8 week roadmap. "Learn Arrays + Graphs (Week 1–2). Build a REST API project (Week 3). Apply to these 10 companies in Week 6." Role-specific, realistic, not generic.

Why it works: this is what users actually pay for. It replaces a ₹10,000 career counsellor.

📈 Progress Tracker NICE TO HAVE V1.1

Check off completed milestones. Re-score resume as skills improve. See your placement probability trend upward. Weekly email nudges.

Why it works: turns a one-time tool into a habit. Critical for retention and paid conversion.

🏆 Shareable Score Card VIRAL LEVER

One-tap shareable card: "My PlacementCoach score: 68% → I'm working on it. Check yours →". Optimized for WhatsApp and Instagram Stories.

Why it works: organic growth loop. Students are competitive. They will share this.

08 / Tech Architecture

⚙ How We Build It

🖥 Frontend
  • Next.js 14 (App Router)
  • Tailwind CSS + shadcn/ui
  • Vercel deployment (free tier → scale)
  • Mobile-first (most users on phone)
🔧 Backend
  • FastAPI (Python) — REST API
  • JWT auth (no OAuth complexity at start)
  • PDF resume parsing: pdfminer / pypdf2
  • Render.com (free → $7/mo starter)
🤖 AI / ML Layer
  • GPT-4o (OpenAI) for resume analysis + action plan generation
  • Custom prompt engineering — India-specific context baked in
  • Rule-based scoring for placement probability (ML later)
  • spaCy / Regex for keyword extraction from JDs
🗄 Database
  • PostgreSQL (Supabase — free 500MB)
  • Redis (for caching resume scores)
  • S3-compatible (Supabase Storage for PDFs)

Team split (2 founders): Founder 1 → Frontend + Backend + deployment. Founder 2 (ML) → Prompt engineering, scoring model, resume NLP. Both use shared Supabase instance.

09 / Data Strategy

📊 Data Moat (Critical)

This is the most important long-term section. The product that owns the most India-specific placement data wins. Here is how to build that moat.

Phase 1 · Bootstrap (Month 1–2)
  • Kaggle placement datasets (starting point only — generic)
  • Manually scrape 500–1000 job descriptions from Naukri, LinkedIn, Internshala
  • Survey 100 recent placed/unplaced students: CGPA, skills, company, outcome
  • Use synthetic data + GPT-4 to augment thin categories
Phase 2 · User Data (Month 3–6)
  • Every user who submits a resume contributes anonymized signal
  • Post-placement survey: "Did you get placed? Which company?" → ground truth
  • A/B test action plan recommendations → measure conversion
  • Build India-specific keyword dictionary per role/domain

Important: Be transparent about data use in privacy policy. Never sell individual data. The moat comes from aggregate patterns, not individual profiles. Make users trust you — that is the product.

11 / Risks & Mitigation

⚠ What Can Kill Us

❌ Risk 1: Bad Predictions Destroy Trust HIGH

If we predict "70% chance of getting placed" and the user doesn't, they feel cheated.

Fix → Always show ranges ("40–65%"), never absolutes. Add disclaimer. Frame it as "current readiness score" not "prediction." Shift messaging to improvement, not prophecy.

❌ Risk 2: One-Time Use / Low Retention HIGH

User checks score, gets action plan, disappears. Never pays.

Fix → Progress tracker + weekly nudges creates a habit. Re-scoring resume as skills improve shows tangible progress. Email drip: "Your score improved 8 points this week."

❌ Risk 3: GPT Costs Too High MEDIUM

At scale, GPT-4o per-resume cost adds up. Free users could drain the budget.

Fix → Rate-limit free tier. Cache action plans by profile similarity. Move toward fine-tuned smaller model (Mistral) once dataset is large enough.

❌ Risk 4: Ethical / Psychological Harm MEDIUM

Telling a student "low placement probability" could cause anxiety or demotivation.

Fix → Always follow a score with an action. Never show a red number without a green roadmap next to it. UX framing matters: "Here's how to improve" not "You're behind."

❌ Risk 5: Market Education Needed LOW

Students may not see the value of paying ₹149 for career guidance vs. free YouTube.

Fix → Free tier must be genuinely useful and create a "wow" moment. Trust is built before asking for money. The score card + resume ATS score is the hook.
